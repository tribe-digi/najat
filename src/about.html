My Story

Why I chose a different kind of medicine

Dr Najat Amharar didn't come to functional medicine by chance — she came to it by necessity.

After years of conventional practice, she saw a recurring pattern: patients with real, debilitating symptoms being told that their tests were "normal" and being handed prescriptions to manage — not resolve — their suffering. That wasn't good enough.

She spent the next several years deepening her training in functional, integrative, and nutritional medicine — learning to read the body as a whole, interconnected system rather than a collection of isolated symptoms.

"I believe your body has an extraordinary capacity to heal. My job is to remove the obstacles standing in its way."

Today, Dr Najat works with patients experiencing complex, chronic health challenges — from hormonal chaos and gut dysfunction to autoimmune conditions, fatigue syndromes, and metabolic imbalances. Her care is unhurried, evidence-based, and deeply personal.

My Approach

The functional medicine difference

Functional medicine asks why — not just what. It's a science-based approach that considers the web of interactions between your genes, environment, lifestyle and mindset.
